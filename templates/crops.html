Crops & Disease Information

Learn about common vegetables, their diseases, symptoms, causes, and preventive measures to help keep your crops healthy and productive.

1. Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)

Common Diseases: Early blight, Late blight, Tomato mosaic virus, Tomato leaf mold, Spider mites.

Symptoms: Yellowing of leaves, dark or brown spots on leaves, stems, or fruits, curling or wilting leaves, reduced fruit yield.

Causes: Fungal or viral infections, high humidity, poor air circulation, contaminated tools.

Prevention & Management: Use resistant varieties, maintain proper spacing, rotate crops, remove infected plants, apply appropriate fungicides or neem oil sprays, avoid overhead irrigation.

2. Potato (Solanum tuberosum)

Common Diseases: Early blight, Late blight, Powdery mildew, Black scurf.

Symptoms: Dark or irregular spots on leaves, tuber rot, wilting of plants, reduced yield.

Causes: Fungal infection, excessive soil moisture, use of infected seed tubers.

Prevention & Management: Use certified disease-free seed, crop rotation, avoid planting in waterlogged fields, apply fungicides during humid periods.

3. Bell Pepper / Capsicum (Capsicum annuum)

Common Diseases: Bacterial leaf spot, Anthracnose, Powdery mildew, Verticillium wilt.

Symptoms: Dark spots on leaves and fruits, leaf curling, yellowing, stunted growth.

Causes: Bacterial or fungal infection, high humidity, poor drainage.

Prevention & Management: Avoid overhead watering, maintain good spacing, remove infected leaves, use copper-based sprays, practice crop rotation.

4. Cabbage (Brassica oleracea var. capitata)

Common Diseases: Black rot, Downy mildew, Clubroot, Fusarium wilt.

Symptoms: Yellowing and wilting of leaves, dark vein discoloration, stunted growth, soft rot at roots.

Causes: Bacterial or fungal infections, contaminated soil, excessive moisture.

Prevention & Management: Use disease-free seedlings, maintain proper spacing, rotate crops, apply fungicides, remove infected plants immediately.

5. Lettuce (Lactuca sativa)

Common Diseases: Downy mildew, Leaf spot, Bacterial soft rot.

Symptoms: Yellow spots on leaves, soft or water-soaked patches, stunted growth.

Causes: Fungal and bacterial infections, waterlogged soil, contaminated tools or seedlings.

Prevention & Management: Improve drainage, avoid overhead irrigation, use resistant varieties, remove and destroy infected leaves, sanitize tools.

6. Eggplant / Brinjal (Solanum melongena)

Common Diseases: Verticillium wilt, Phomopsis blight, Bacterial wilt, Anthracnose.

Symptoms: Yellowing of leaves, wilting, dark lesions on stems or fruits, reduced yield.

Causes: Soil-borne fungal or bacterial pathogens, poor soil drainage.

Prevention & Management: Crop rotation, use disease-free seedlings, apply fungicides, avoid water stagnation around plants, remove and destroy infected plants.

7. Spinach (Spinacia oleracea)

Common Diseases: Downy mildew, Leaf spot, Fusarium wilt.

Symptoms: Yellowing leaves, white or brown spots, wilting, poor growth.

Causes: Fungal infection, high humidity, contaminated seeds.

Prevention & Management: Use treated seeds, ensure good air circulation, water in the morning, remove infected plants promptly.

8. Carrot (Daucus carota)

Common Diseases: Alternaria leaf blight, Root rot, Powdery mildew.

Symptoms: Brown spots on leaves, decay of roots, reduced size and yield.

Causes: Fungal infection, waterlogged or poorly drained soil, contaminated seeds.

Prevention & Management: Proper drainage, crop rotation, remove infected roots, use fungicide if necessary.

9. Cauliflower (Brassica oleracea var. botrytis)

Common Diseases: Black rot, Downy mildew, Clubroot.

Symptoms: Yellowing leaves, brown spots, soft roots, malformed heads.

Causes: Fungal or bacterial infection, contaminated soil, excessive moisture.

Prevention & Management: Use disease-free seedlings, maintain proper spacing, rotate crops, apply fungicides, remove infected plants.
